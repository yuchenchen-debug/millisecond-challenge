## 9. Hacker News

### Show HN Post

**Title:** Show HN: The Millisecond Challenge – How precise is your sense of time?

**First Comment (by submitter):**

I built a simple browser game that tests your time perception accuracy across 5 rounds (1s, 2s, 3s, 5s, 10s). You stop a running timer as close to the target as possible, and the game measures your total error in milliseconds.

Tech: Pure vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, no framework. Firebase for the global leaderboard. Hosted on GitHub Pages.

Some design decisions:

- **Rank tiers** from SSS (<10ms total error) to F, with increasingly savage commentary. The psychological hooks are intentional — the gap between ranks is designed to feel "almost reachable" to keep you playing.
- **Fake world record** at 7ms to give players something to chase.
- **Per-round taunts** that react to your accuracy in real time (under 5ms and it accuses you of being a robot; over 200ms and it suggests you were playing with your eyes closed).
- Mobile-first responsive design with JetBrains Mono for the timer display.

The most interesting finding: people's accuracy on the 10-second round is consistently worse proportionally than the 1-second round, suggesting we lose temporal precision over longer intervals. Would be curious if anyone has an academic reference for this.

Feedback welcome, especially on the UX and the rank thresholds.

## 10. Product Hunt

### Tagline

Stop a timer at exactly the right second. Get ranked. Get roasted.

### Description

**The Millisecond Challenge** tests your sense of time across 5 rounds (1s, 2s, 3s, 5s, 10s). Stop a running timer as close to the target as possible. Get ranked SSS to F. Read what the game thinks of your performance.

**Why it's fun:**
- SSS rank requires <10ms total error — only 0.01% can do it
- Every rank comes with increasingly savage commentary
- Per-round taunts react to your accuracy in real time
- Shows what % of players you beat
- "One more try" addiction loop is very real

**Why it's interesting:**
- Pure vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, no framework
- Explores human time perception and how it degrades over longer intervals
- The psychological hooks (near-miss to next rank, taunting, percentile) are deliberately designed

Free, no account needed, works on mobile and desktop.

### Maker's First Comment

Hi PH! I built this because I was curious how precise human time perception really is.

Turns out: not very. Most people land in C-D rank on their first try, which means >500ms total error across 5 rounds. The game is deliberately designed to make each rank feel *just* out of reach — you'll see "only 0.012s away from S rank!" and immediately hit play again.

The taunting messages are the secret sauce. Nobody wants to accept being told "a monkey pressing randomly might be more accurate than you." So they play again. And again.

Some stats from the first week:
- Average session: 7+ attempts
- Most common rank: C
- Number of verified SSS scores: 0

Would love feedback on the rank thresholds and whether the difficulty curve feels right. Also open to adding new round types if there's interest (reverse countdown, random targets, etc.)
